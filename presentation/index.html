This year Linux celebrates 30 years from it’s beginning.

Linux logo

Let's make a quick chronology of the most (and not so) important Linux events.

The man behind of Linux named Linus Torvalds. That start coding on his grandfather’s computer Commodore VIC-20, when he was 11-12 years old and this computer used BASIC. Linus says that according to his sister Sara, the first code he showed her was a two-line “GOTO” program in BASIC. He tried to show-off his new programming skills on the Commodore by making it repeat “Sara is the best.”

Linus Torvalds and his brother together

On August 25th, 1991, 21 years old Linus, student of Helsinki University posted the now famous Usenet message announcing he was building a free operating system which in his words were:

“just a hobby, won’t be big and professional like gnu”.

Young Linus Torvalds

Torvalds first published the Linux kernel under its own licence, which had a restriction on commercial activity. It was with version 0.12 in February’1992 that Linux Kernel was released under GPL.

"Making Linux GPLed was definitely the best thing I ever did."

Linus Torvalds.

The oldest version of Linux is Slackware Linux version 1.0 that was released on 17th July of 1993 and it is still in development.

Another important date in the history of Linux is 1997 when GNOME Project was born. GNOME is one of the most successful open source projects. It has been crucial to the spread of desktop Linux. It has given us a number of programs that we use today on desktop Linux and a year after in 1998 KDE 1.0 released. Another crucial part of desktop Linux.

Next one is not a historical day, but I guess it should be mentioned. The 15th of February 1999 all linux users observed Windows Refund Day. As most of the computer system came bundled with Microsoft Windows, Linux users around the world went to Microsoft’s offices to return their unused licenses of Windows.

15th February, 1999, "Windows Refund Day"

In 2000 Steve Jobs offered Linus Torvalds to come work for Mac OS to make “Unix for the biggest user base”. Also, Jobs even tried to persuade Linus to drop working on Linux.Obviously, Linus refused the offer. The next year Microsoft CEO Steve Ballmer said about Linux: “Linux is a cancer that attaches itself in an intellectual property sense to everything it touches”.

In 2002 the first commercial Linux for Business IT was released. RHEL(Red Hat Enterprise Linux) is one of the few Linux distributions that changed Linux forever.

Linus shows his home office in 2016

On 20th October 2004, Ubuntu 4.10 was released. This new Linux distribution marketed itself as Linux for human being. Ubuntu made it easier for normal users to install and use Linux. With Ubuntu, Linux reached to the non-geek domain. Thank you, Mark Shuttleworth (the founder and CEO of Canonical, the company behind the development of the Linux-based Ubuntu operating system.).

Shuttleworth on board the International Space Station. The second self-funded space tourist and the first-ever South African in space.

For years, software BitKeeper was used for version control in Linux Kernel development. When in 2005, the founder of BitKeeper decided to not provide the free version to the community anymore. Linus Torvalds decided to work on his own version control system and thus Git was born. By the way, BitKeeper was open sourced in 2016 but I guess it’s was too late.

The first step for Linux in the world of mobile OS happened in 2008, with the release of Android version 1.0, a mobile operating system based on Linux Kernel. While desktop Linux might not have been that big success, with Android, Linux is dominating the world of mobile OS.

In 2010: Red Hat became first billion dollar open source company. The success of Red Hat breaks the myth that open source companies cannot make money.

Who could forget the iconic image of Linus Torvalds showing the middle finger to NVIDIA and literally saying it “f*ck you!”. And surprisingly, this helped to force NVIDIA to work on providing better support for Linux.

Linus Torvalds during a Q&A session at Aalto University in Finland.

In 2014 Microsoft’s new CEO Satya Nadella brought a shift in Microsoft policies and for the first time Microsoft started open sourcing its products and bringing it to Linux. Also Ubuntu claims 22,000,000 users.

So, we can definitely say that in the 30 years since Linux was launched, that operating system – has become the most important open source software project in history, powering all supercomputers, serving as the backbone of the cloud and internet, running a majority of mobile phones and mainframe computers, and even operating on the International Space Station.

"Most good programmers do programming not because they expect to get paid or get adulation by the public, but because it is fun to program."

Linus Torvalds.
